Vivariums and Whats so Great About Them.

Learn About Nature and Ecosystems

Ecosystems are complicated and more difficult than caring for a potted plant. A vivarium contains an entire ecosystem that is able to support itself with little help from the one maintaining the system. In order to setup a mini container containing the cycle of life, it is necessary to know what will be happening in said container. They consist of substrate, drainage layer, various plant life, a cleanup crew (bugs) to remove waste, and of course a beloved small pet. All of the life is essential to the rest, and it's easy for things to go wrong and become out of balance. Mold can grow excessively if the area is too damp, certain plants may not be compatible with the choosen climate or with each other, and the bugs that eat the waste may die out.

Care for Other Living Beings

Caring for pets is proven to have several health benefits according to the CDC such as a decrease in blood pressure, anxiety, feelings of loneliness, and symptoms of PTSD. The enclosure primarily needs to care for the animal inhabiting it, but it's not the only living thing being cared for. The plant life, and cleanup crew play an important part in upholding a livable environment along with the pet too. Your pet will have a natural home similar to one out in nature.

A piece of nature in your home

Another benefit to terrariums/vivariums is that they are pleasing to look at and makes great decor. Not only is it pleasing to look at but it makes for great visual interest as its not just static piece of furniture. Because the system is alive, everyday it will look a little bit different, and theres always a little critter to observe. It's like having a little piece of nature in your home, which can feel rejuvinating. It's unique and can be a conversation piece when having people over.
